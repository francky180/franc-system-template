## Purple Cow — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Product design, positioning, standing out in crowded markets.

### Remarkable or Invisible
- A purple cow is the only one in a field of brown ones. People stop and stare.
- If your product is not remarkable, no marketing budget will save it.
- Being safe is the riskiest thing.

### Built into the Product
- Remarkability has to be designed into the product, not bolted on later.
- Ads can't create remarkability. They can only amplify it.
- Spend on product, not on ads, in the early days.

### Find the Otaku
- Otaku = people who are passionate about a category and tell others.
- Build for them first. They will spread the word for free.
- Don't aim for mass appeal — aim for the obsessive few.

### The Sliver of the Market
- Don't try to please everyone. Pick a specific slice and delight them completely.
- The pleased few become evangelists.
- Average products for average people are the most expensive marketing of all.

### Practical Rules
- Take risks. Boring is invisible. Boring fails.
- Differentiation must be visible, talkable, story-worthy.
- Test and iterate. Most "remarkable" ideas miss. The one that works pays for the rest.
- The world has too many brown cows. Be purple or be ignored.

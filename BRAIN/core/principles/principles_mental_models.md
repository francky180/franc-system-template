## Mental Models — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Decisions across any domain, debugging thinking, learning new fields fast.

### First Principles
- Strip every problem down to its physical truths. Don't reason by analogy when stakes are high.
- Ask "what do we actually know is true here?" before "what do other people do?"
- First principles let you outcompete people who only copy.

### Systems Thinking
- Look at the whole system, not the part. Optimizing one node can break the others.
- Feedback loops drive everything — find the reinforcing and balancing loops.
- Stocks vs flows — distinguish levels (cash, users) from rates (cash burn, signups/day).

### Probability & Risk
- Expected value > most likely value. A small chance of huge upside beats a large chance of small upside.
- Asymmetric bets — risk small, gain big. Avoid the inverse at all costs.
- Tail risks dominate long-term outcomes. Hedge against catastrophic loss above all.

### Useful Models
- Compound interest — small advantages applied over time crush short-term wins.
- Marginal cost vs marginal value — only the next unit matters for decisions.
- Regression to the mean — extreme results don't last. Don't bet on continued outliers.
- Diminishing returns — first hour of effort beats the tenth. Reallocate when curves flatten.
- Game theory — your moves affect others' moves. Think iterated, not one-shot.

### Practical Use
- Build a personal latticework — collect models from many fields. Cross-apply.
- When stuck, switch the model. A new lens often reveals the answer.
- Beware models that always work — they probably don't. All models simplify; some are useful.

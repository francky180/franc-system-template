## Your Money or Your Life — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Spending decisions, career choices, evaluating tradeoffs, designing financial independence.

### Money = Life Energy
- Money is what you trade your life energy for. Every purchase costs hours of your life.
- Calculate your real hourly wage — subtract commute, work clothes, decompression, taxes. The number is shocking.
- Ask of every purchase: "is this worth this many hours of my life?"

### The Financial Independence Path
- Track every dollar in and out. No exceptions for a few months. The truth is in the data.
- Calculate fulfillment per dollar — does this expense match my values? Cut what doesn't.
- The crossover point — when investment income exceeds expenses, work becomes optional.

### Spending Wisdom
- Enough is a number, not a feeling. Define yours.
- More stuff doesn't mean more happiness past a baseline. The hedonic treadmill is real.
- Question every category — housing, transport, food. Big wins live in big categories.

### Work & Purpose
- Your job is not your life. It's the tool that buys your time back.
- Don't conflate income with worth. Many high earners live broke; many low earners live rich.
- Once you have enough, choose work for meaning, not money. That choice is freedom.

### Practical Habits
- Visualize the wall chart — income vs expenses vs investments — every month. Make progress visible.
- Frugality is not deprivation — it's matching spending to actual value.
- Live below your means and the means become the freedom.

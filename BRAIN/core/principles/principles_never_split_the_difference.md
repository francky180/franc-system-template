## Never Split the Difference — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Negotiating prices, salary, contracts, partnerships, customer disputes.

### Tactical Empathy
- Understand the other side's emotions and perspective fully — they don't have to be wrong for you to be right.
- Demonstrate understanding before pushing your view.
- Empathy is leverage, not weakness.

### Mirroring
- Repeat the last 1-3 words of what the other person said. They will elaborate.
- Mirrors create connection and surface information.
- Use them when you don't know what to say next.

### Labeling
- Name the emotion you see — "It seems like you're frustrated about timing."
- Labels diffuse negative emotions and reinforce positive ones.
- Wrong labels still work — the person will correct you and reveal more.

### Calibrated Questions
- Ask "how" and "what" questions. Avoid "why" (sounds accusatory).
- "How am I supposed to do that?" puts the problem in their lap without saying no.
- "What about this works for you?" surfaces real interests.

### The Power of No
- Getting "no" is starting the negotiation, not ending it. People feel safe saying no.
- Design questions that invite a "no" — "Is now a bad time to talk?" works better than "Do you have a minute?"
- People defend nos more than they defend yeses.

### The Black Swan
- There's always information you don't have that would change everything.
- Hunt for it. Listen for inconsistencies and clues.
- The best negotiators uncover what others miss.
- Never split the difference — find creative solutions where both sides win, not lazy compromises where both lose.

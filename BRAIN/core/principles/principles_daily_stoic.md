## The Daily Stoic — Core Principles

**When to apply:** Every task. This is the operating system mindset — how to think, act, and respond.

### The Four Stoic Virtues
1. **Wisdom** — Know what matters and what doesn't. Don't waste energy on things outside your control.
2. **Courage** — Do what's right even when it's hard. Ship the work, have the hard conversation, make the call.
3. **Justice** — Treat people fairly. Do good work. Don't cut corners.
4. **Temperance** — Self-control. Don't overreact, don't overdo, don't overthink. Measure twice, cut once.

### Key Stoic Principles
- **Focus on what you can control** — Your actions, your effort, your response. Not outcomes, not other people.
- **Obstacle is the way** — Every problem is training. The bug is the lesson. The failure is the feedback.
- **Amor fati** — Love your fate. Don't resist what happens. Use it.
- **Memento mori** — Remember you will die. Urgency creates focus. Don't waste time.
- **Ego is the enemy** — The moment you think you know everything, you stop learning.
- **Stillness is the key** — Clarity comes from calm, not chaos. Think before you act.
- **Do the work** — Philosophy without action is just talk. Execute.
- **Don't explain, demonstrate** — Results speak. Excuses don't.
- **It's not what happens to you, it's how you respond** — Control your reaction.
- **Stick to the process** — Trust the system. Don't get distracted by shiny objects.

### Applied to Work
- Don't complain about bugs — fix them.
- Don't overthink the plan — execute the first step.
- Don't chase perfection — ship, then iterate.
- Don't react emotionally to errors — diagnose, fix, move on.
- Every task is practice for mastery — treat it that way.

## The Courage to Be Disliked — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Caring too much what others think, seeking validation, stuck in approval loops.

### Separation of Tasks
- Whose task is it? Yours or theirs?
- Other people's opinions of you are their task, not yours.
- Don't try to control what isn't your task. Don't let them control what is.

### Freedom is Being Disliked
- Wanting to be liked by everyone is a cage. Being willing to be disliked is freedom.
- Some people will never like you no matter what. That's fine.
- Living for others' approval is not living.

### The Past Doesn't Determine You
- Adler rejects determinism. The past is interpretation, not chains.
- Trauma doesn't decide your future. Your meaning-making does.
- Choose the meaning that empowers you forward.

### Community Feeling
- Real happiness comes from contribution to others, not from being praised.
- Find your community and contribute. The contribution is the reward.
- Don't seek recognition. Seek to be useful.

### All Problems are Interpersonal
- Almost all suffering involves other people.
- Free yourself from comparison and approval-seeking.
- Choose horizontal relationships (equal) over vertical ones (above/below).

### Practical Rules
- Today is the only day you can change. Don't wait.
- Live with the courage to be normal. You don't have to be special.
- Stop seeking recognition. Start contributing.
- The courage to be disliked is the courage to be free.

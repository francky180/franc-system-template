## Turning Pro — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Career transitions, building discipline, escaping the amateur mindset.

### Amateur vs Pro Mindset
- The amateur is afraid. The pro acts despite fear.
- The amateur waits for permission. The pro grants it to themselves.
- The amateur identifies with their feelings. The pro identifies with their work.

### Habits of the Pro
- Show up every day.
- Show up no matter what.
- Stay on the job all day.
- Commit over the long haul.
- Demand real stakes from yourself.
- Accept payment. Real money proves real work.
- Maintain professional reserve.

### Addictions and Distractions
- Amateurs hide from their work in addictions — drama, social media, food, stories.
- Each addiction is a way to avoid the real work.
- Identify yours. Cut them.

### Self-Identification
- The amateur identifies with the small self — fears, ego, validation needs.
- The pro identifies with the larger self — the work, the craft, the calling.
- Identity drives behavior. Change identity to change behavior.

### Turning Pro is Free
- Costs nothing. Just a decision.
- The decision changes everything that follows.
- Make it once and rebuild your life around it.

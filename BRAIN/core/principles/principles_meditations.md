## Meditations — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Daily morning prep, dealing with frustration, leadership pressure, ego checks.

### Control What You Can
- You have power over your mind, not outside events. Realize this and you find strength.
- Don't waste energy on what you can't control. Spend it all on what you can.
- The obstacle in the path becomes the path.

### Memento Mori
- You could leave life right now. Let that determine what you do, say, and think.
- Life is short. Don't waste it on petty grievances or postponed action.
- Death gives meaning to today's decisions.

### Other People
- When you encounter someone difficult, remember they too will die. They are doing what their nature demands.
- Don't be ruled by what others think. Most people don't even know what they think.
- The best revenge is to be unlike the person who wronged you.

### Inner Citadel
- Your mind is a fortress no one can take without your permission.
- Hold judgment. Events don't disturb you — your opinions about them do.
- "It looks like rain" — the events. "And that's terrible" — your story.

### Daily Practice
- At dawn, prepare yourself: today I shall meet ungrateful, arrogant, lying, jealous people. None of this surprises me.
- Review the day at night — what did I do well? What can I improve?
- Do good. Expect nothing. Move on.
- The happiness of your life depends on the quality of your thoughts. Mind your thoughts.

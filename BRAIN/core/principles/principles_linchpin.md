## Linchpin — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Career strategy, doing remarkable work, escaping commodity work.

### Become Indispensable
- A linchpin is the person without whom things fall apart. Become one.
- The factory worker is replaceable. The linchpin is not.
- Position your contribution so removing you costs more than keeping you.

### Art is Everywhere
- Art isn't just paint on canvas. It's any human gift given that changes someone.
- Bring art to whatever you do — writing emails, leading meetings, building products.
- The world is hungry for art and starved for it.

### Emotional Labor
- The hardest, most valuable work is emotional labor — caring, deciding, connecting, risking.
- Most workers refuse it because it's uncomfortable. That's why it's valuable.
- Lean into the discomfort.

### The Resistance
- The lizard brain (resistance) screams to keep you safe. It will sabotage every brave act.
- Recognize it for what it is — fear pretending to be wisdom.
- Ship anyway.

### Ship the Work
- The only thing that matters is shipping. Unshipped work is invisible.
- Perfectionism is the resistance in disguise.
- Set deadlines. Hit them. Ship even when scared.

### Practical Rules
- Refuse to be a cog. Don't let the system shape you into average.
- Give gifts at work — value beyond what's transactional. People remember givers.
- Stop following maps. Make new ones.
- The art you make is your real career. The job is just where you make it.

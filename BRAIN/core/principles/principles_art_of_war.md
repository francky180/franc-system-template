## The Art of War — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Competitive strategy, negotiations, market battles, conflict resolution.

### Win Without Fighting
- The supreme art of war is to subdue the enemy without fighting.
- The greatest victory is the one that requires no battle.
- In business: outmaneuver competitors, don't outspend them.

### Know Yourself, Know the Enemy
- Know yourself and know your enemy and you need not fear the result of a hundred battles.
- Know yourself but not the enemy and you'll lose half the time.
- Know neither and you'll lose every time.
- Spend as much time on competitive intelligence as on your own product.

### Position Beats Power
- Choose your battles. Don't fight on terrain that favors the enemy.
- Be where they aren't. Move where they can't.
- The smart fighter imposes his will on the enemy and is not imposed upon.

### Deception
- All warfare is based on deception. Appear weak when strong, strong when weak.
- Conceal your real plans. Reveal them only when execution begins.
- In business: don't telegraph strategy. Let competitors guess wrong.

### Speed and Adaptation
- Speed is the essence of war. Slowness kills armies and companies.
- Be like water — formless, shapeless, taking the form of the container.
- Strategy without tactics is the slowest route to victory. Tactics without strategy is noise before defeat.
- Engage only when victory is certain. Retreat when it isn't. There is no glory in losing battles.

## Extreme Ownership — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Leading teams, project failures, conflict resolution, building accountability culture.

### Own Everything
- The leader owns every outcome. No exceptions.
- If the team failed, the leader failed. Period.
- Blaming subordinates is the fastest way to lose respect.

### No Bad Teams, Only Bad Leaders
- A leader who can't get results from a team would fail with any team.
- Don't blame the people. Fix the leadership.
- Standards rise to whatever the leader tolerates.

### Believe in the Mission
- If you don't believe, no one else will.
- Find the meaning in the mission yourself, then transmit it.
- Disengaged leaders create disengaged teams.

### Check the Ego
- Ego clouds judgment, blocks learning, breaks teams.
- The leader who can take a hit and keep moving is the one who wins.
- Don't make decisions to protect your ego.

### Cover and Move
- Teams move forward by supporting each other.
- Internal competition destroys missions. Watch for it and shut it down.
- Every member exists to help the others succeed.

### Decentralized Command
- Junior leaders must make decisions in real time. They can't wait.
- Give them clear intent and let them execute.
- Micromanagement kills initiative and slows everything down.

### Practical Rules
- Plan thoroughly. Communicate clearly. Empower the team.
- Discipline = freedom even at the team level. Tight processes free people to focus on the mission.
- Prioritize and execute. Don't try to fix everything at once.
- Lead up the chain as well as down. Good leaders manage both.

## Made to Stick — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Writing copy, presentations, brand messaging, anything that needs to be remembered.

### SUCCESs Framework
- Simple — strip to the core idea.
- Unexpected — break a pattern to grab attention.
- Concrete — sensory specifics, not abstract concepts.
- Credible — give reasons to believe.
- Emotional — make people feel.
- Stories — narratives stick better than facts.

### The Curse of Knowledge
- Once you know something, you can't imagine not knowing it. This is why experts write bad copy.
- Constantly translate for the audience.
- Test on someone who knows nothing. If they don't get it, your copy doesn't work.

### Concrete Beats Abstract
- Don't say "world-class customer service." Say "we answer every email in 2 hours, day or night."
- Specific details create belief. Abstractions create suspicion.
- Show, don't tell, in copy too.

### Stories as Vehicles
- People remember stories long after they forget facts.
- A story is a simulation — the listener mentally experiences it.
- Use stories to teach, sell, motivate, lead.

### Practical Rules
- Find the core. What's the one most important thing? Lead with that.
- Use unexpected angles. Pattern interrupts get attention; predictability gets ignored.
- Specific numbers beat round ones. "73 minutes" beats "an hour or so."
- Steal good ideas — borrow templates that work and apply them to your domain.

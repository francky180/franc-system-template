## Building a StoryBrand — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Website copy, brand messaging, pitch scripts, simplifying confused marketing.

### Customer is the Hero
- Your brand is not the hero. The customer is. You are the guide.
- Never position yourself as the protagonist. Position yourself as the experienced helper.
- Examples: Yoda to Luke, Gandalf to Frodo. The guide has wisdom and a plan.

### The 7-Part Framework
- A character (the customer) — who are they?
- Has a problem (external, internal, philosophical) — what's wrong?
- Meets a guide (you) — who has empathy and authority.
- With a plan — concrete steps.
- Calls them to action — direct or transitional.
- Ends in success — show the outcome.
- Or failure — show the cost of inaction.

### Three Levels of Problem
- External — the surface problem (e.g., losing weight).
- Internal — how it makes them feel (e.g., insecure, unworthy).
- Philosophical — why it matters in a bigger sense (e.g., everyone deserves to feel confident).
- Address all three. Most brands stop at external.

### Clarity Beats Cleverness
- If you confuse, you lose. Customers will pick the clearest option, not the cleverest.
- Strip jargon, internal language, and "creative" copy.
- The grandma test — could a 12-year-old understand your homepage in 5 seconds?

### Practical Application
- Create a one-liner — character + problem + plan + success.
- Lead with what the customer wants, not what you offer.
- Make every CTA crystal clear. Don't bury the next step.
- Repeat the message everywhere. Consistency beats novelty in branding.

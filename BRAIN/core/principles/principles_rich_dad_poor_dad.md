## Rich Dad Poor Dad — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Money decisions, business design, evaluating purchases, building income streams.

### Assets vs Liabilities
- Assets put money in your pocket. Liabilities take it out. Most "assets" people own are actually liabilities.
- Your house is not an asset until it generates cash flow. Stop pretending it is.
- Buy assets first, luxuries second. Let assets pay for luxuries.

### The Rat Race
- Most people work for money. The rich make money work for them.
- Higher salary creates higher lifestyle creates more dependency. Escape by building cash-flowing assets.
- Job income is taxed first, spent second. Business income is spent first, taxed second. Structure matters.

### Mindset Shifts
- Don't say "I can't afford it" — ask "how can I afford it?" Forces creative problem solving.
- Fear and greed are the two emotions wrecking financial decisions. Notice them, don't act on them.
- The poor and middle class work for money. The rich learn so money works for them.

### Action Principles
- Mind your own business — your real business is your asset column, not your job.
- Pay yourself first — invest in assets before bills. Force yourself to find the bill money.
- Work to learn, not to earn — early career, optimize for skills you can monetize forever.
- Don't be afraid to fail fast and cheap — most lessons come from real mistakes.

### Financial IQ
- Know accounting, investing, markets, and law. These four together make you dangerous.
- Track where your money actually goes. Most people have no idea.
- Tax knowledge is wealth knowledge. The system rewards entrepreneurs and investors.
- The only way to get rich is to keep more of what you make, not just earn more.

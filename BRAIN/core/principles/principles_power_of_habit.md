## The Power of Habit — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Building or breaking habits, designing user retention, changing team behavior.

### The Habit Loop
- Cue → routine → reward. Every habit follows this pattern.
- To change a habit, identify the cue and reward, then change the routine in between.
- You can't extinguish habits, only replace the routine.

### Cues
- Five categories: location, time, emotional state, other people, immediately preceding action.
- Find which cue fires your habit. Most people don't know.
- Track for a week — what triggers the behavior?

### Cravings
- Habits stick because the brain starts craving the reward when the cue appears.
- Sell the craving, not the product. Pepsodent succeeded by creating the craving for tingle, not by selling clean teeth.
- In marketing: identify the unmet craving, then offer the routine.

### Keystone Habits
- Some habits unlock cascading change — exercise, daily journaling, family dinners.
- Find the keystone in your life. Change it and other things change automatically.
- In business: a keystone process change can reshape culture.

### Belief
- Belief is the ingredient that makes a reshaped habit permanent.
- Groups make habit change stick because they create belief that change is possible.
- AA works because of group belief, not the program steps.
- Once you believe change is possible, it usually is.

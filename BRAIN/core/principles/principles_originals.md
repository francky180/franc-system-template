## Originals — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Pitching new ideas, fighting for innovation, building creative teams, breaking conformity.

### Originality is Learnable
- Original thinkers aren't born with mystical talent. They use specific habits.
- Anyone can be more original by changing approach, not waiting for genius.
- Inputs matter. Sources matter. Habits matter.

### Volume Beats Selection
- Original thinkers produce massive volume. Most of their ideas are bad.
- The hits look like genius only because the misses are forgotten.
- Generate many ideas. The best ones emerge from quantity.

### Procrastination Helps
- Strategic procrastination gives time for incubation. The mind keeps working.
- Done immediately = first idea wins. First ideas are usually obvious.
- Delay finishing — but start early.

### Vouch for Familiarity
- Novel ideas feel risky. Familiarity reduces resistance.
- Expose people to your idea repeatedly before asking for buy-in.
- Mere exposure shifts opinion in your favor.

### Defaults Are Often Wrong
- Question every default. Most rules exist because nobody questioned them.
- The phrase "we've always done it this way" is the enemy.
- Originals challenge defaults instinctively.

### Practical Rules
- Build a portfolio. Don't bet on one idea — make many bets.
- Find allies inside the system. Originals need supporters.
- Vuja de — look at familiar things as if for the first time.
- The best originals are willing to be wrong frequently to be right occasionally.

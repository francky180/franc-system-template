## The Republic — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Ethical decisions, leadership style, questioning systems, designing organizations.

### Question Everything
- The unexamined life is not worth living. Test every belief.
- What you assume to be obvious is often wrong. Examine the foundations.
- Truth comes from dialogue, not declaration.

### Justice as Internal Order
- Justice is each part of the soul doing its proper work — reason ruling, spirit serving, appetite controlled.
- Personal order precedes external order.
- A just person is harmonized within first.

### The Cave
- Most people live among shadows, mistaking them for reality.
- Once you see the truth, return to help others — even though they will resist.
- The path out of the cave is painful. Truth blinds before it illuminates.

### Philosopher Kings
- The best leaders are those who don't want power but accept it out of duty.
- Those who chase power are usually unfit for it.
- Wisdom and power must be united, or governance fails.

### Practical Lessons
- Tyranny grows from unchecked appetite. The undisciplined soul becomes the undisciplined state.
- Education shapes the soul more than any law.
- Beware the persuasive speaker — rhetoric without truth is poison.
- Build your character first; everything else follows from that.

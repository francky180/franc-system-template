## Mindset — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Learning new skills, dealing with failure, leading others, self-talk.

### Two Mindsets
- Fixed mindset — abilities are innate. Failure proves you're inadequate.
- Growth mindset — abilities develop through effort. Failure proves you're learning.
- The mindset determines the trajectory.

### Praise Effort, Not Talent
- Telling someone "you're so smart" creates fixed mindset.
- Telling them "you worked hard on that" creates growth mindset.
- Praise the process, not the outcome or the trait.

### Failure as Feedback
- Growth mindset reframes failure as data.
- "Not yet" is the magic phrase. You haven't done it yet — keep going.
- Every failure is a step toward mastery, not proof of inability.

### Avoid the Validation Trap
- Fixed mindset chases validation — likes, awards, comparisons.
- Growth mindset chases improvement.
- The validation chase makes you fragile and uncompetitive.

### Practical Application
- When you catch yourself saying "I can't" — add "yet."
- Embrace challenges. They're the only path to growth.
- Effort is the path to mastery, not a sign you lack talent.
- The view you adopt for yourself profoundly affects the way you lead your life.

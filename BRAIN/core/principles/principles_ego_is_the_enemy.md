## Ego is the Enemy — Key Principles

**When to apply:** When you start winning (most dangerous), when you fail, when you're learning, when you're about to make a big decision.

### Ego Sabotages Every Stage
- Aspire — ego makes you talk instead of work.
- Success — ego makes you complacent and arrogant.
- Failure — ego makes you blame others and quit.
- Watch for ego in all three phases. It looks different but kills the same way.

### Talk Less, Do More
- The empty barrel makes the most noise. The work is the only credential that matters.
- Don't tell people what you're going to do. Show them.
- Energy spent talking is energy not spent doing.

### Become a Student Forever
- Pick up a new mentor at every stage. Always be in learning mode.
- The moment you think you know enough, you're done growing.
- Egos hate being taught. Crush yours by inviting teachers.

### Restrain Yourself
- The hardest thing is to do nothing when ego demands attention.
- Don't seek credit. Don't pick fights with critics. Don't broadcast your wins.
- Quiet confidence beats loud bragging every time.

### Manage Yourself
- Detach from outcomes. Focus on the process.
- Don't believe your own press — good or bad.
- The work is all that matters. Everything else is noise.

### Practical Rules
- When you succeed, double your discipline. Success is the most dangerous moment.
- When you fail, take responsibility. Don't blame; learn.
- The pursuit of excellence does not require self-glorification.
- Ego is the enemy of what you want and of what you have.

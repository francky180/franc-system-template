## Give and Take — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Networking, hiring, relationship building, designing reciprocity-based systems.

### Three Reciprocity Styles
- Takers — try to get more than they give. Win short-term, lose long-term.
- Matchers — keep score, equal exchange. Stable middle.
- Givers — help without expectation. Found at both top and bottom of success.

### The Giver Paradox
- Givers are at the bottom AND the top of success rankings. The middle is matchers and takers.
- Bottom givers — exhausted, exploited, unfocused.
- Top givers — strategic, generous in ways that create asymmetric returns.
- The difference is sustainability, not generosity.

### Five-Minute Favors
- Help others in ways that cost you little but mean a lot to them.
- Introductions, advice, connections, recommendations — all five-minute favors.
- Stack them. They compound into a network of allies.

### Spot the Takers
- Takers self-promote, take credit, appear charming to superiors and harsh to subordinates.
- Watch how people treat those who can't help them. That's their real character.
- Don't give to takers — they take and don't reciprocate. Match them instead.

### Givers Win in Networks
- In a connected world, reputation travels fast. Givers build long-term reputation.
- Takers get found out eventually, then suffer.
- Givers create goodwill that returns at unexpected times.

### Practical Rules
- Be a giver by default but match takers. Don't be naive.
- Don't burn out. Strategic giving requires energy management.
- Find ways to give that align with your strengths. They cost you less, mean more.
- The best giving doesn't keep score. The returns come anyway.

## The 4-Hour Workweek — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Designing lifestyle, building muse businesses, automating, outsourcing.

### DEAL Framework
- Definition — define what you actually want. Not money, but experiences.
- Elimination — cut the time wasters. Apply 80/20 ruthlessly.
- Automation — outsource and systemize what remains.
- Liberation — escape geography. Mobility is freedom.

### 80/20 Everywhere
- 20% of effort produces 80% of results. Find the 20%.
- 20% of clients produce 80% of profit and 80% of headaches. Sometimes the 20s overlap.
- Cut the bottom 80% of effort and double down on the top 20%.

### Parkinson's Law
- Work expands to fill the time available. Set tighter deadlines.
- A 2-hour project becomes a week if you give it a week.
- Compress to force focus.

### Mini-Retirements
- Don't save retirement for the end. Take mini-retirements throughout life.
- Travel young. The deferred life plan often defers forever.
- Time and mobility now beat money for "someday."

### The Muse
- Build a small, automated business — a muse — that funds your lifestyle without consuming your time.
- Start narrow, test small, automate fast.
- The goal isn't a big business. It's a free life.

### Practical Rules
- Eliminate before delegating. Automate before hiring.
- Information diet — most news is irrelevant noise.
- Batch communications. Don't check email all day.
- Lifestyle design beats career planning. Design what you want, then reverse-engineer.

## Thinking in Bets — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Strategic decisions, evaluating risk, learning from past choices, separating skill from luck.

### Decisions Are Bets
- Every decision is a bet on an uncertain future.
- Stop asking "right or wrong." Ask "what are the odds and what's the expected value?"
- Embracing uncertainty is the start of better decisions.

### Resulting is the Trap
- Don't judge a decision by its outcome. Judge it by the quality of the process.
- A good decision can have a bad outcome and vice versa.
- Outcomes have luck baked in. Process is what you control.

### Calibrated Confidence
- Express confidence in percentages, not certainty.
- "I'm 70% sure" is more honest and more useful than "I'm right."
- Calibration improves with practice and feedback.

### Truthseeking Pods
- Surround yourself with people who care about truth more than agreement.
- Reward the messenger of bad news, not punish them.
- Without challenge, your beliefs go uncorrected.

### Practical Rules
- Track decisions and outcomes separately. Review for process, not result.
- Pre-commit to changing your mind under specific conditions.
- Use 10-10-10 thinking — how will I feel about this in 10 minutes, 10 months, 10 years?
- Mental time travel — imagine your future self looking back. What would they want you to do?

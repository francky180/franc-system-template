## Your Next Five Moves — Core Principles

**When to apply:** Planning projects, making business decisions, evaluating opportunities, competitive strategy.

### The 5 Moves Framework
1. **Know yourself** — Understand your strengths, weaknesses, and what you actually want. Every decision starts with self-awareness.
2. **Master reasoning** — Think through problems from multiple angles. Study the other side. Anticipate consequences.
3. **Master building a team** — You can't scale alone. Find people who complement your weaknesses.
4. **Master strategy** — Think 5 moves ahead. What happens after you do this? And then what? And then?
5. **Master power plays** — Know when to be aggressive, when to wait, when to walk away.

### Strategic Thinking Rules
- **Before every decision, ask: "And then what?"** — Trace the chain of consequences 3-5 steps out.
- **Speed of processing > speed of action** — Think fast, act deliberately.
- **Study the greats in your industry** — Don't reinvent what already works. Model success, then innovate.
- **Bet on yourself, but hedge your bets** — Bold moves yes, but never all-in on one thing.
- **Your competitors tell you what works** — Study what they do. Do it better or differently.
- **Data beats opinion** — Test, measure, then decide. Gut feeling is for tiebreakers only.
- **The right move at the wrong time is the wrong move** — Timing matters more than tactics.
- **Create leverage before you need it** — Build options, relationships, and assets before the negotiation.
- **Know your numbers** — Revenue, costs, margins, conversion rates. If you can't measure it, you can't improve it.
- **The best move is often the one nobody expects** — Predictability is a disadvantage.

## Pitch Anything — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Sales pitches, investor presentations, high-stakes meetings, client proposals.

### Frame Control
- Whoever owns the frame owns the conversation. Lose the frame, lose the deal.
- Frames clash on contact. The stronger frame wins instantly.
- Don't react to their frame — break it with a stronger one.

### The STRONG Method
- Set the frame
- Tell the story
- Reveal the intrigue
- Offer the prize
- Nail the hook
- Get the deal
- Each step has a purpose. Skipping any breaks the pitch.

### Status
- Pitching from low status guarantees failure. The audience won't take low-status pitches seriously.
- Establish status early — credentials, audacity, refusal to be patronized.
- Local star power — be the alpha in the room you're in.

### Time Pressure
- Long pitches lose. The brain tunes out after 20 minutes.
- Keep it tight. End before they want you to.
- Time pressure increases perceived value — slow pitches feel desperate.

### Avoid Neediness
- Neediness is the deal killer. The moment you appear to need the deal, you lose it.
- Be willing to walk. The willingness is the leverage.
- Three signals of neediness: validation seeking, slow movement, retreating from challenge.

### The Hot Cognition Stack
- Engage the prospect's primitive brain — novelty, intrigue, emotion, urgency.
- Logic justifies what hot cognition decides.
- Make them feel the deal before they think it.

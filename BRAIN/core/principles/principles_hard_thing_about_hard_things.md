## The Hard Thing About Hard Things — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Crisis decisions, leading through uncertainty, layoffs, near-death company moments.

### There Are No Easy Answers
- The hard thing isn't setting big goals. The hard thing is laying people off when you miss them.
- Most management books teach the easy stuff. The hard stuff has no formula.
- When there's no good move, you make the least-bad one.

### CEO Psychology
- The struggle is when self-doubt becomes self-hatred. Everyone goes through it. Don't quit.
- Tell yourself there's nothing wrong with you. The job is hard, period.
- Find someone to talk to. Isolation amplifies everything.

### Hire for Strength, Not Lack of Weakness
- Don't hire someone who's good at everything. Hire someone who's great at what you need.
- Every candidate has weaknesses. Pick the ones you can tolerate.
- Strengths build companies, not the absence of weakness.

### Wartime vs Peacetime CEO
- Peacetime — focus on growth, culture, expansion. Process matters.
- Wartime — focus on survival. Speed and decisiveness beat everything.
- Most CEOs are good at one mode and bad at the other. Know which you're in.

### Hard Truths
- Take care of people, products, and profits — in that order. Get it wrong and the whole thing collapses.
- Layoffs done badly poison the survivors. Layoffs done well preserve trust.
- Tell people the truth — they can handle it. They can't handle being lied to.
- The quality of decisions you make under pressure is what defines you as a leader.

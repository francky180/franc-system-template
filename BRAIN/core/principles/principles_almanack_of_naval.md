## The Almanack of Naval Ravikant — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Career strategy, wealth building, mental clarity, choosing what to work on.

### Wealth vs Money vs Status
- Wealth is assets that earn while you sleep.
- Money is how we transfer time and wealth.
- Status is your rank in the social hierarchy.
- Wealth is the only positive-sum game. Status is zero-sum. Don't confuse them.

### Specific Knowledge
- Specific knowledge is the kind you can't be trained for — found by following genuine curiosity.
- It feels like play to you and work to others.
- Schools teach generic knowledge. The internet rewards specific knowledge.

### Leverage
- Three forms of leverage: labor, capital, and code/media.
- Labor and capital are permission-based. Code and media are permissionless.
- Build leverage you don't need to ask for. That's where outsized returns live.

### Play Long-Term Games
- Play long-term games with long-term people.
- Compounding is in everything — knowledge, relationships, wealth, reputation.
- Avoid people who play short-term games. They will burn you eventually.

### Happiness
- Happiness is a default state when you remove the sense that something's missing.
- Desires are contracts you make with yourself to be unhappy until you get what you want.
- Pick one big desire at a time. Multiple desires create suffering.

### Practical Code
- The secret to career: don't try to get noticed. Do incredible work that people can't ignore.
- Read what you love until you love to read.
- Free time + free will is the ultimate wealth.
- Be patient. The goal is to retire from things you don't want to do, not to retire from doing things.

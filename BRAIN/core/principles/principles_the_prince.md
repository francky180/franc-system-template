## The Prince — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Leading organizations, navigating politics, making hard choices, holding power without losing it.

### Acquisition vs Maintenance
- Acquiring power and keeping it require different skills. Many conquerors fail at ruling.
- Don't assume the same approach that won the deal will keep the customer.
- After taking ground, focus on consolidation before more conquest.

### Feared vs Loved
- It is better to be feared than loved if you cannot be both. But avoid being hated.
- Love is fickle; fear endures. Yet hate brings destruction.
- In business: respected and slightly intimidating beats friendly and pushed around.

### Cruelty Used Well
- If injuries must be done, do them all at once. Spread cruelty stretches resentment.
- Benefits should be distributed slowly, so they're savored.
- Bad news in one move; good news in many.

### Appearances
- Men judge more by appearance than by reality. Manage how you appear deliberately.
- Be virtuous when possible, ruthless when necessary, but always appear virtuous.
- Reputation outruns reality in the short term; align them in the long term.

### Fortune and Virtù
- Fortune controls half of our actions, but the other half is ours.
- Virtù — boldness, decisiveness, adaptability — bends fortune.
- The bold defeat the timid even when fortune favors neither.

### Practical Rules
- Avoid flatterers. They corrupt judgment. Surround yourself with people willing to tell you the truth.
- Never disarm your subjects unless you can guarantee their loyalty.
- The wise prince adapts to changing circumstances. The fool clings to one method until it kills him.
- New states require new measures. Don't apply old rules to new situations.

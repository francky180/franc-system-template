## Games People Play — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Stuck in repeating arguments, dealing with manipulators, recognizing your own patterns.

### Three Ego States
- Parent — rules, judgments, "shoulds." Inherited.
- Adult — rational, present, fact-based. Goal state.
- Child — emotional, reactive, primal. Needs and feelings.
- Healthy interactions are Adult to Adult. Most dysfunction is Parent-Child crossed.

### Recognizing Games
- Games are repetitive social patterns with hidden agendas and predictable bad endings.
- If you keep ending up in the same fight or feeling, you're in a game. Find your role.
- Common games: "Why don't you / Yes but" — asks for help, rejects every offer, wins at being unhelpable.
- "If it weren't for you" — blames others for self-imposed limits.
- "Now I've got you" — sets a trap to express anger with justification.

### Stopping Games
- Refuse to play. Drop your role and the game collapses.
- Move to Adult state — name the pattern, ask what the person actually needs.
- Don't take the bait emotionally. Games need emotional fuel from the other side.

### In Business
- Watch for manipulation games in negotiations and partnerships.
- "Schlemiel" — makes a mess and demands forgiveness. Don't enable.
- "Kick me" — sets up rejection so they can complain about it. Don't kick.
- Customers, employees, and partners all play games. Recognize them and respond as Adult.

### Self-Application
- Map your repeating life patterns. Each is likely a game you're playing with yourself.
- Your payoff is usually emotional, not practical. Find what feeling you're chasing.
- Replace games with intimacy — honest, direct, vulnerable communication. Higher reward, higher risk.

## Blue Ocean Strategy — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Choosing markets, pricing, positioning, escaping commoditization, designing differentiated offerings.

### Red vs Blue Oceans
- Red oceans — existing markets with bloody competition. Margins compress, innovation slows.
- Blue oceans — new markets with no competition. You set the rules.
- Stop fighting in red oceans. Create blue ones.

### Value Innovation
- Pursue differentiation AND low cost simultaneously. Conventional strategy says you must choose. Blue ocean rejects that.
- Make the competition irrelevant by serving needs no one else does.
- Value innovation = buyer value goes up, costs go down.

### Four Actions Framework
- Eliminate — what factors should be removed entirely?
- Reduce — what should be reduced well below industry standard?
- Raise — what should be raised well above standard?
- Create — what should be offered that the industry has never offered?
- Apply this matrix to any commoditized market.

### Six Paths
- Look across alternative industries — what serves the same job differently?
- Look across strategic groups within your industry.
- Look across the chain of buyers — sell to the influencer, not the buyer, sometimes wins.
- Look across complementary offerings — bundle solutions to bigger problems.
- Look across functional vs emotional appeal — flip the dimension.
- Look across time — anticipate trends and ride them early.

### Execution
- Get the strategic sequence right — buyer utility, price, cost, adoption — in that order.
- Tipping point leadership — find the people who will swing the rest. Convert them first.
- Build execution into the strategy from day one. Most strategy fails at implementation, not idea.

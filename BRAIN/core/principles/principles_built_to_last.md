## Built to Last — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Building a company you want to last decades, designing culture, balancing change and continuity.

### Clock Building, Not Time Telling
- Build organizations that outlast you, not personal hero stories.
- Focus on the institution, not the genius founder. The company should run without depending on one person.
- Process and culture are the time-tellers — design them deliberately.

### Genius of the AND
- Visionary companies reject "either/or" thinking. They embrace BOTH purpose AND profit, stability AND change, conservatism AND innovation.
- Don't accept tradeoffs that aren't really tradeoffs.
- Find ways to have both, not pick one.

### Core Ideology + Progress
- Preserve the core, stimulate progress.
- Core values stay fixed. Practices, products, strategies change constantly.
- Know which is which — confusing them kills companies.

### BHAGs
- Big Hairy Audacious Goals — long-term, vivid, energizing missions that demand bold action.
- A BHAG should be just outside your current capability. If it's safe, it's not big enough.
- BHAGs align teams better than quarterly targets ever do.

### Cult-Like Cultures
- Visionary companies have strong cultures that fit and exclude. Not for everyone — and that's the point.
- Indoctrinate new hires. Make values explicit. Reward fit, exit misfit fast.
- Tightness on values, looseness on methods.
- Try a lot, keep what works. Most of what looks like strategy was actually evolution from many experiments.

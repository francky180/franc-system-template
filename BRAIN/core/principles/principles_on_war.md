## On War — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Long competitive campaigns, navigating uncertainty, leading through resistance.

### War is Politics by Other Means
- War is the continuation of policy by other means. Never lose sight of the political end.
- In business: strategy serves a goal. Don't fight for its own sake.
- The objective shapes the means, not the reverse.

### Friction
- Everything in war is simple, but the simplest thing is difficult.
- Friction is the difference between war on paper and war in reality. Plan for it.
- Small frictions accumulate into major delays. Build buffers everywhere.

### The Trinity
- War is shaped by three forces — passion (people), chance (commander), reason (government).
- Imbalance in any one breaks the strategy.
- Apply to any organization — emotion, judgment, logic must all be present.

### The Center of Gravity
- Identify the enemy's center of gravity — the source of their strength.
- Strike there, not at peripheral targets.
- In business: a competitor's center might be a key customer, a key talent, a flagship product.

### Will and Endurance
- Moral forces matter as much as material ones. Belief in victory drives victory.
- The longer the war, the more will matters. Resources run out; will doesn't have to.
- Keep your forces concentrated. Avoid the temptation to disperse and defend everything.
- The defensive is the stronger form of war. Attack only when you can win decisively.

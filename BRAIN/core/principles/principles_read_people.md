## How to Read People Like a Book — Core Principles

**When to apply:** Understanding what Franc really wants (read between the lines), writing copy that connects emotionally, client communication, UX decisions.

### Reading Body Language (Digital Equivalent)
- **Speed of response** = interest level. Fast replies = engaged. Delays = hesitation or disinterest.
- **Word choice reveals emotion** — Urgent language = frustrated. Casual = confident. Excessive detail = anxiety.
- **What they don't say matters most** — Gaps in what someone tells you reveal what they're avoiding.
- **Clusters, not singles** — One signal means nothing. Multiple signals in the same direction = truth.

### Emotional Decoding
- **People communicate what they feel, not what they think** — Respond to the emotion, not just the words.
- **Mirror to build rapport** — Match energy, pace, and tone. People trust those who feel similar.
- **Detect incongruence** — When words and behavior don't match, behavior is the truth.
- **Defensive signals** — Short answers, redirections, topic changes = discomfort. Address the underlying issue.

### Intention Reading
- **People reveal their priorities through action, not words** — Watch what they do, not what they say they'll do.
- **Baseline first, then deviations** — Understand someone's normal pattern before judging changes.
- **Self-interest drives everything** — Everyone acts in their own interest. Find their real motivation.
- **The best communicators ask questions** — You can't read someone if you're doing all the talking.

### Applied to AI Work
- When Franc says "do you understand" — he's checking alignment. Confirm clearly, don't start executing.
- When Franc says "make it clean" — he means professional, minimal, no clutter, no extra.
- When Franc interrupts — the current approach is wrong. Stop, don't push through.
- When Franc says "don't break anything" — he's had things broken before. Verify before and after.

## Letters from a Stoic — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Time decisions, relationship management, emotional discipline, dealing with loss.

### Time is the Most Valuable Asset
- We are not given a short life — we make it short. We waste it.
- Hold every hour in your grip. Defend time from the people who would steal it.
- Money lost can be recovered. Time lost cannot.

### Anger and Calm
- The greatest remedy for anger is delay. Wait before responding.
- Anger is short madness. Don't let it make decisions.
- He who is afraid of his own anger is afraid of his own weakness.

### Friends and Influence
- Choose your friends carefully — you become an average of them.
- Avoid the crowd. Their habits become yours.
- Withdraw to clearer minds when surrounded by mediocrity.

### Wealth and Desire
- It is not the man who has too little, but the man who craves more, that is poor.
- Practice being content with less so you can never be impoverished.
- The greatest wealth is a poverty of desires.

### Practical Wisdom
- Begin at once to live, and count each separate day as a separate life.
- Postpone nothing. The present is all you have.
- Sometimes even to live is an act of courage.
- He who studies with a philosopher should take away with him some good thing every day.

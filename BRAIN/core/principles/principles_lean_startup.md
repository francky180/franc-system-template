## The Lean Startup — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Building products, validating ideas, deciding when to pivot, allocating dev time.

### Validated Learning
- The unit of progress in a startup is validated learning, not features shipped.
- Every assumption is a hypothesis. Test the riskiest one first.
- Build → Measure → Learn. Then loop. Speed of loops beats size of loops.

### Minimum Viable Product
- The MVP is the smallest thing that lets you learn, not the smallest version of the product.
- A landing page, a video, a manual service — all valid MVPs if they test the hypothesis.
- Don't build features users don't need. Don't build at all if you can fake it.

### Pivot vs Persevere
- If your hypothesis fails, pivot. If it's working, persevere.
- A pivot is a structured change to test a new fundamental hypothesis.
- Pivot examples: zoom in, zoom out, customer segment, customer need, platform, business architecture, value capture, growth engine, channel, technology.
- The longer you wait to pivot, the harder it gets.

### Innovation Accounting
- Track real metrics, not vanity metrics. Active users, retention, conversion rates — not signups and downloads.
- Measure cohort behavior over time, not aggregate growth.
- A good metric tells you what to do next.

### Engines of Growth
- Sticky — customers stay; growth comes from retention.
- Viral — customers bring more customers.
- Paid — customer LTV exceeds CAC.
- Pick one and optimize. Trying all three at once means none works.

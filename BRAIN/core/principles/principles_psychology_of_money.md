## The Psychology of Money — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Investment decisions, evaluating financial advice, behavioral finance traps.

### Behavior Beats Knowledge
- Doing well with money has little to do with how smart you are. It has everything to do with how you behave.
- The smartest investors crush themselves with bad behavior. Discipline beats IQ.
- Nobody is crazy — financial decisions make sense in the personal experience that shaped them.

### Compounding & Time
- Time is the most powerful force in finance. The longer you stay in the game, the bigger the swing.
- Buffett's wealth came from 75 years of returns, not high returns. Survival enables compounding.
- The biggest gains come from doing nothing for very long periods.

### Wealth vs Riches
- Wealth is what you don't see — the money not spent. Riches is the visible stuff that loudly signals broke.
- Spending money to show people how much money you have is the fastest way to have less.
- The highest form of wealth is the ability to wake up and say "I can do whatever I want today."

### Risk & Survival
- Plan on the plan not going to plan. Always have margin of safety.
- Tail events drive everything — a few decisions account for most outcomes. Stay in the game to catch them.
- Never risk what you have and need for what you don't have and don't need.

### Practical Rules
- Be reasonable, not rational. A reasonable plan you can stick to beats a rational one you abandon.
- Save for save's sake. You don't need a goal — flexibility is the goal.
- Avoid the extremes of any decision. They lead to regret.
- Define the game you're playing. Don't take cues from people playing different games.

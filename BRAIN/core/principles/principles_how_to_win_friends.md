## How to Win Friends and Influence People — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Sales, networking, hiring, leading people, customer service.

### Don't Criticize, Condemn, or Complain
- Criticism puts people on the defensive and rarely changes behavior.
- Even hardened criminals blame everyone but themselves. Don't expect different from your team.
- Find another way to redirect — questions, examples, indirect feedback.

### Give Honest Appreciation
- Real, specific praise is rare. Use it generously.
- Don't flatter — flattery is fake. Find what's actually good and name it.
- People rarely tire of being appreciated.

### Arouse the Other Person's Want
- Talk in terms of what they want, not what you want.
- The only way to influence someone is to find what they care about and connect to it.
- Sell your prospect a hole, not a drill.

### Make People Feel Important
- Become genuinely interested in other people. Most are starved for interest.
- Smile. Use the person's name often. Ask about their life.
- Be a good listener. Encourage them to talk about themselves.

### Win People to Your Way of Thinking
- Avoid arguments. The only way to win an argument is to avoid it.
- If wrong, admit it quickly and emphatically.
- Begin in a friendly way. Get them saying yes, yes immediately.
- Let the other person feel that the idea is theirs.

### Lead Without Resentment
- Begin with praise and honest appreciation. Then mention mistakes indirectly.
- Talk about your own mistakes before criticizing the other.
- Ask questions instead of giving direct orders.
- Let the other person save face. Praise the slightest improvement and praise every improvement.

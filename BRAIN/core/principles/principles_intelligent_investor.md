## The Intelligent Investor — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Stock investing, evaluating any asset, distinguishing investment from gambling.

### Investment vs Speculation
- An investment is one which, upon thorough analysis, promises safety of principal and adequate return. Anything else is speculation.
- Most "investors" are speculators with better PR. Know which one you are.
- If you can't tell the difference, you're a speculator.

### Mr. Market
- Imagine the market as a moody business partner who quotes you a price every day. Some days he's manic, some days depressed.
- You don't have to trade with him. You can ignore his quotes and look at the underlying business.
- Use his moods, don't let them use you. Buy when he panics, sell when he's euphoric.

### Margin of Safety
- Never pay more than 2/3 of what an asset is worth based on conservative analysis.
- The margin of safety protects against errors in judgment. Always assume you might be wrong.
- The bigger the margin, the more mistakes you survive.

### Defensive vs Enterprising
- Defensive investor — diversified index funds, simple, low maintenance. Best for most people.
- Enterprising investor — willing to do work, hunt undervalued opportunities. Higher reward, higher effort.
- Don't pretend to be enterprising if you're not willing to do the homework.

### Discipline
- The investor's chief problem — and even his worst enemy — is likely to be himself.
- Patience is the only edge most retail investors can have. Use it.
- Never invest in something you don't understand. Period.
- The market in the short run is a voting machine; in the long run, a weighing machine.

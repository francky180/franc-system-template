## Essentialism — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Calendar overload, decision fatigue, scope creep, overwhelm.

### Less But Better
- Essentialism is not about getting more done — it's about getting only the right things done.
- The disciplined pursuit of less. Most productivity advice adds; essentialism subtracts.
- If it isn't a clear yes, it's a clear no.

### Explore Then Eliminate
- Explore many options. Then ruthlessly cut to the essential few.
- Most people skip exploration and miss better choices, or skip elimination and drown.
- Apply to projects, commitments, possessions, relationships.

### The Power of No
- Every yes is a no to something else. Make the trade explicit.
- A graceful no preserves the relationship while protecting your time.
- Say no slowly and pleasantly, but firmly.

### Buffers and Boundaries
- Build buffers into estimates, schedules, finances. Overoptimism kills.
- Set boundaries that protect what matters from what doesn't.
- People will respect the boundaries you defend.

### Subtract to Add
- Editing your life is more powerful than adding to it.
- The Italian sculptor who sees the statue inside the marble — your life works the same way.
- Cut what isn't the statue.

### Practical Rules
- Use the 90% rule — if it's not a 9 or 10, it's a 0. Stop saying yes to 6s.
- Build routines that automate the essentials. Free decision energy for the few important calls.
- Progress beats perfection. Done beats perfect.
- The way of the essentialist is the path of less but better.

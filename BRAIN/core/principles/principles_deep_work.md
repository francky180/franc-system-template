## Deep Work — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Designing your work day, prioritizing focus, eliminating distractions, building rare skills.

### The Deep Work Hypothesis
- Deep work — focused, undistracted work on cognitively demanding tasks — is becoming rare and increasingly valuable.
- Those who cultivate it will thrive. Those who don't will be commodity workers.
- The two core abilities of the new economy: master hard things fast, and produce at elite level.

### Shallow vs Deep
- Shallow work — emails, meetings, admin. Necessary but commoditized.
- Deep work — what creates value, builds skills, advances your edge.
- Most professionals spend 80% on shallow and wonder why they don't grow.

### Schedule Deep Work
- Block deep work time on the calendar like meetings. Defend it ruthlessly.
- Decide in advance: when, where, how long, on what. Don't decide in the moment.
- Rituals beat motivation. Same time, same place, same setup.

### Embrace Boredom
- Don't reach for your phone every idle moment. Train your mind to handle stillness.
- The ability to concentrate is built through deliberate practice — including practicing not being distracted.
- Schedule internet time. Live offline by default.

### Drain the Shallows
- Quantify the depth of every task. If a smart college grad could do it, it's shallow.
- Set a fixed end time for the workday. Forces prioritization.
- Say no aggressively. Every yes to shallow is a no to deep.
- Become hard to reach. Email is for senders' convenience, not yours.

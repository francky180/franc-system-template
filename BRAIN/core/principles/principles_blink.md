## Blink — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Fast decisions, hiring, reading rooms, evaluating people quickly.

### Thin-Slicing
- Trained intuition can read situations in seconds and be highly accurate.
- Experts make snap judgments based on patterns invisible to novices.
- The first few seconds often contain most of the signal.

### Train Your Intuition
- Snap judgments are only reliable in domains where you have deep experience.
- In unfamiliar areas, slow down. Trust intuition only where you've earned it.
- Experts develop intuition through deliberate practice over years.

### Less is Sometimes More
- Too much information can hurt decisions, especially under uncertainty.
- The best diagnostic doctors used three pieces of data, not thirty.
- Cut to the signal. Ignore the noise.

### Beware Bias
- Snap judgments inherit your biases — racial, gender, attractiveness, height.
- Test your gut against blind data when you can.
- Awareness of bias is the first defense against it.

### Practical Application
- Use thin-slicing where you have expertise. Slow down where you don't.
- For high-stakes decisions, validate intuition with data.
- Train intuition by getting fast feedback in your domain.
- The conscious mind is overrated. The trained unconscious is underrated.

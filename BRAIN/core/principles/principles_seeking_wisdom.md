## Seeking Wisdom — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Investing, big bets, partnership decisions, strategic planning, learning.

### Avoid Stupidity First
- It's remarkable how much long-term advantage you get by trying to be consistently not stupid. Smart is overrated.
- Invert — figure out what would cause failure, then don't do that.
- If you can't explain it simply, you don't understand it. Demand clarity from yourself and others.

### Multidisciplinary Thinking
- Use models from many fields — physics, biology, economics, psychology. The toolkit beats the specialist.
- The man with a hammer sees every problem as a nail. Carry many hammers.
- Combine models — answers usually live at intersections, not within a single discipline.

### Incentives Drive Behavior
- Show me the incentive and I'll show you the outcome. Always check who benefits.
- Never ask a barber if you need a haircut. Trust advice less when the advisor profits.
- Create incentive systems carefully — people will game whatever you measure.

### Learning from Mistakes
- Other people's mistakes are cheaper than your own. Read history, biographies, post-mortems.
- Keep a decision journal — write what you decided and why. Review later.
- The most important things in life cannot be measured. Don't ignore them because they're hard.

### Practical Wisdom
- Margin of safety — assume things will go wrong. Build buffers.
- Circle of competence — know the edge of what you understand. Stay inside it for big bets.
- Charlie Munger's rule: never compete with a fanatic. They will outwork you on something they care about more.
- Patience is the rarest competitive advantage. Most people can't sit and wait.

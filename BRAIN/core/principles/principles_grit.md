## Grit — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Long-term goals, plateaus, when motivation fades, building endurance.

### Grit = Passion + Perseverance
- Talent is overrated. Effort counts twice.
- Talent x effort = skill. Skill x effort = achievement. Effort shows up twice in the equation.
- Perseverance over years beats brilliance in moments.

### Find Your Top Goal
- Map your goals into a hierarchy. The top is the why; the bottom is daily actions.
- Cut goals that don't ladder up to the top one.
- Grit comes from clarity about what you're working toward.

### Deliberate Practice
- Improvement comes from focused practice on weakness, not just doing more of what you're good at.
- Set stretch goals. Get feedback. Reflect. Refine. Repeat.
- Most "experience" doesn't make you better. Deliberate practice does.

### Find Purpose
- Purpose is the belief that your work matters to others.
- Connect your daily work to a larger meaning.
- Without purpose, perseverance runs out of fuel.

### Optimism and Hope
- Pessimists see setbacks as permanent. Optimists see them as temporary.
- Hope is not a feeling, it's a posture — "tomorrow will be better because of what I do today."
- Train hope as a skill.

### Practical Rules
- Pick one hard thing and stick with it for years. Most people quit too early.
- The "Hard Thing Rule" — finish what you start, no quitting on a bad day.
- Surround yourself with gritty people. Their grit becomes yours.
- The grit you build in one domain transfers to others.

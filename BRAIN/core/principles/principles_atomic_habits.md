## Atomic Habits — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Building new habits, breaking bad ones, designing systems, improving consistency.

### Systems Beat Goals
- You do not rise to the level of your goals. You fall to the level of your systems.
- Goals are good for direction, systems are good for progress.
- Forget the goal, focus on the system that produces it.

### Identity-Based Habits
- The most effective way to change is to change who you think you are.
- Don't say "I'm trying to quit smoking." Say "I'm not a smoker."
- Every action is a vote for the type of person you want to become.

### The Four Laws (To Build)
- Make it obvious — design environment cues. Visible defaults to wins.
- Make it attractive — pair with something you enjoy. Bundle.
- Make it easy — reduce friction. Two-minute rule: shrink the habit to two minutes.
- Make it satisfying — reward yourself immediately. Brain learns what feels good.

### The Four Laws (To Break)
- Make it invisible — remove cues. Hide the phone, the snacks, the trigger.
- Make it unattractive — link it to negative consequences.
- Make it difficult — add friction. Even 20 seconds extra kills bad habits.
- Make it unsatisfying — add accountability or visible cost.

### Compound Rules
- 1% better every day = 37x better in a year. Tiny gains compound enormously.
- Habits compound — both good and bad. Neutral days don't exist.
- Plateau of latent potential — nothing seems to work for a long time, then suddenly everything does. Don't quit at the plateau.
- The valley of disappointment kills more progress than effort ever does.

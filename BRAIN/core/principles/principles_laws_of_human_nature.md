## The Laws of Human Nature — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Reading people, hiring, partnership decisions, dealing with difficult personalities, self-awareness.

### Master Yourself First
- The greatest prison is the one in your own mind. Most reactions are unconscious.
- Observe yourself like a stranger — catch your patterns before they catch you.
- Emotional self-awareness is the foundation of reading others. You can't read what you can't see in yourself.

### Reading People
- Words lie. Body language and pattern of behavior tell the truth. Watch what people do over time, not what they say.
- Past behavior is the best predictor of future behavior. Don't believe the new story; check the old patterns.
- People reveal themselves in small moments — tone, pauses, micro-reactions. Pay attention to the edges.

### Dark Patterns to Watch
- Envy — disguised as moralizing or backhanded compliments. Identify enviers and starve them of fuel.
- Grandiosity — people who need to feel special are unstable allies. Avoid attaching to their identity.
- Aggression — passive or active, it leaks. Notice it early.
- The shadow self — everyone has repressed traits that surface under stress. Anticipate them.

### Influence & Connection
- Make people feel seen — most are starved for genuine attention. This alone wins loyalty.
- Listen with full presence — most people fake listening. Real attention is rare and powerful.
- Adapt to people's character — flex your style to theirs to be heard.

### Long Game
- Think long-term in all relationships. Short-term wins create long-term enemies.
- Cultivate patience and detachment — react less, observe more.
- Develop a sense of purpose larger than ego. It immunizes against most human petty patterns.
- Embrace your shadow — own your worst impulses so they don't run you.

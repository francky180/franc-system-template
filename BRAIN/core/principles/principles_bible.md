## The Bible — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Ethical decisions, character building, dealing with adversity, leadership, long-term planning, integrity tests.

### Character & Integrity
- Your word is your bond — let your yes be yes and your no be no. In business: never promise what you can't deliver.
- The measure you use will be measured back to you — treat clients, vendors, and competitors how you want to be treated.
- A good name is worth more than riches — protect reputation above profit.
- Pride goes before destruction — humility prevents the fall. Stay teachable.
- Walk with the wise and become wise — your inner circle determines your trajectory.

### Discipline & Work
- Whatever your hand finds to do, do it with all your might — half-measures are wasted motion.
- The diligent prosper, the slothful starve — consistent action compounds.
- Do not despise small beginnings — every empire starts as a seed.
- Count the cost before you build — plan resources before committing.
- The borrower is slave to the lender — debt is bondage. Avoid it where possible.

### Wisdom & Decisions
- In the multitude of counselors there is safety — get advice before big moves.
- The simple believe everything, the prudent give thought to their steps — verify before you trust.
- Better a patient person than a warrior — restraint is strength.
- Hear the matter fully before answering — listen first, judge second.
- A soft answer turns away wrath — de-escalate with calm, not heat.

### Purpose & Long View
- Store treasures that don't decay — invest in skills, relationships, and reputation, not just money.
- Do not worry about tomorrow — focus on today's work; tomorrow handles itself.
- To everything there is a season — read the timing. Don't force what isn't ready.
- The race is not to the swift but to the steady — endurance beats speed.
- What does it profit a man to gain the world and lose his soul — never trade who you are for what you want.

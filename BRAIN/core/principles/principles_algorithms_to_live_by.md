## Algorithms to Live By — Key Principles

**When to apply:** When to stop searching, how to schedule work, when to commit, how to prioritize a queue.

### Optimal Stopping
- 37% rule — explore the first 37% of options without committing, then take the next one better than all you've seen. Apply to hiring, dating, apartment hunting.
- Look-then-leap balances regret of stopping too early vs too late.

### Explore vs Exploit
- Early in any timeline, explore widely. Late in any timeline, exploit what works.
- The interval determines the strategy. New job = explore. Last year before retirement = exploit.
- Try new things while you have time to recover from bad picks.

### Sorting & Searching
- Don't over-sort what you barely use. Searching is often cheaper than sorting.
- Bucketing beats fine ordering for most real life problems — by category, not strict order.

### Scheduling
- Earliest due date minimizes lateness. Shortest processing time minimizes pile-up.
- Context switching has a cost — batch similar tasks. Single-tasking wins.
- Thrashing — when you spend all time switching and none working. Pick one. Finish it.

### Caching
- Recent and frequent items belong on top. Mirror this in tools, files, mental queues.
- Forgetting isn't failure — it's optimal cache eviction.

### Decision Heuristics
- Predict less when data is thin — use simple rules, avoid overfitting your life plan.
- Randomness as strategy — when stuck in local optima, randomize your next move.
- Computational kindness — design choices to make others' decisions easier. Offer 3 options, not 30.

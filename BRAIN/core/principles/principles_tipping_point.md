## The Tipping Point — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Launching products, spreading ideas, building movements, scaling adoption.

### The Three Rules
- Law of the Few — a small number of people drive most of the spread.
- Stickiness Factor — the message must stick in people's minds.
- Power of Context — small environmental cues make huge differences.

### The Three Roles
- Connectors — people with huge networks. They link disparate groups.
- Mavens — information specialists. They know everything about a topic and tell others.
- Salesmen — natural persuaders. They convince doubters.
- Identify and recruit all three for any campaign.

### Stickiness
- Make your message memorable, repeatable, and impossible to ignore.
- Small tweaks in framing, packaging, or sequence can dramatically change stickiness.
- Test different versions until one sticks.

### Power of Context
- People are more sensitive to context than personality.
- Small environmental cues — broken windows, clean subways — change behavior at scale.
- Design the environment, not just the message.

### Tipping Point Mechanics
- Adoption curves are non-linear. Nothing happens, nothing happens, then everything happens.
- The last unit pushed before the tipping point gets all the credit. The first units did most of the work.
- Don't quit before the tipping point.

### Practical Application
- Find your few influencers. Convert them first.
- Design messages that stick — concrete, surprising, emotional.
- Engineer the context for adoption. Reduce friction at every step.
- Small things, done right, can produce massive change.

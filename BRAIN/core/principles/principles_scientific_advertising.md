## Scientific Advertising — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Writing direct response copy, A/B testing, evaluating marketing performance.

### Test Everything
- Advertising is salesmanship in print. Treat it as a science: hypothesis, test, measure.
- Never argue about what works. Run a test.
- The cheapest education is paid by other people's failed campaigns. Read direct response history.

### Specific Beats General
- Specific claims convince. Vague boasts don't.
- "1,247 dentists agree" beats "experts agree."
- Numbers, names, dates, places — all signal truth.

### Sell the Benefit
- People don't buy products. They buy improvements in their lives.
- Lead with what the customer gets, not what the product is.
- Every feature must answer "so what?" The answer is the benefit.

### Headlines Decide Everything
- The headline picks the audience. A good ad with a bad headline reaches no one.
- Promise the reader something they want. Or arouse curiosity that demands resolution.
- Test 10 headlines before settling. Winners often surprise you.

### Practical Methods
- Use coupons, codes, dedicated landing pages — anything that makes results trackable.
- Track cost per result, not impressions or clicks alone.
- Sample offers, free trials, money-back guarantees all reduce risk and lift conversion.
- The principles from 1923 still work. Human nature doesn't change much.

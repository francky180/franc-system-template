## The Intelligence Trap — Key Principles

**When to apply:** When you're sure you're right, expert decisions, detecting your own blind spots.

### The Trap
- High intelligence doesn't immunize you from bias — it lets you build better rationalizations for bad ideas.
- Smart people are harder to correct because they out-argue feedback.
- Expertise narrows perception — you see only what your training prepared you to see.

### Antidotes
- Intellectual humility — explicitly acknowledge what you don't know on every topic.
- Cultivate "active open-mindedness" — actively seek and weigh evidence against your view.
- Curiosity beats certainty — frame inquiry as questions, not conclusions.

### Practical Defenses
- Pre-commit to changing your mind under specific conditions. Write them down.
- Seek out the strongest version of the opposing view. Steelman, don't strawman.
- Slow down on confident conclusions. The strongest feeling of being right is the biggest warning.

### Wisdom Habits
- Practice "epistemic courage" — say "I don't know" out loud. Models humility for others.
- Distinguish facts, inferences, and feelings — keep them in separate buckets.
- Reflect on past mistakes — keep a "wrong list" to remind yourself of overconfidence patterns.
- The smartest person in the room asks the most questions, not gives the most answers.

## Mastery — Core Principles

**When to apply:** Learning new skills, approaching complex tasks, long-term career strategy, quality of work.

### The Path to Mastery
1. **Discover your calling** — Find the work that aligns with your natural inclinations. The thing you'd do even if nobody paid you.
2. **Apprenticeship phase** — Submit to learning. Observe, absorb, practice. No shortcuts.
3. **Skill acquisition** — Deliberate practice on fundamentals. 10,000 hours isn't enough — it must be focused, intentional repetition.
4. **Mentorship** — Find someone who has done what you want to do. Absorb their decades of experience.
5. **Creative-active phase** — Once fundamentals are internalized, experiment. Combine skills in new ways.
6. **Mastery** — Intuition develops. You see patterns others can't. Decisions become instinctive.

### Key Principles
- **Resist the temptation of shortcuts** — There is no hack for deep skill. Put in the work.
- **Embrace the pain period** — Confusion and frustration mean you're learning. Comfort means stagnation.
- **Social intelligence matters** — Technical skill alone isn't enough. Read people, navigate politics, build alliances.
- **See the bigger picture** — Connect dots across domains. The best ideas come from combining unrelated fields.
- **Never stop being a student** — Mastery is a process, not a destination. Stay hungry.
- **Transform yourself through practice** — You become what you repeatedly do. Every task is training.
- **The finger pointing at the moon** — Don't confuse the method with the goal. Adapt methods, keep the vision.

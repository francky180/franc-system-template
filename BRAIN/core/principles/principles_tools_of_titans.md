## Tools of Titans — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Building routines, learning from top performers, optimizing health and mind.

### Morning Routines Matter
- Top performers protect the first 60-90 minutes of the day.
- Common elements: hydration, movement, meditation, journaling, no email.
- The morning sets the tone for the rest of the day.

### Test Everything on Yourself
- Don't trust generic advice. Try it. Measure it. Keep what works for you.
- Self-experimentation produces the most reliable rules.
- Track meaningful metrics, not vanity ones.

### Fear-Setting
- Fear is often the strongest signal of what you should do.
- Define your fears precisely. Most of them shrink under examination.
- The cost of inaction is usually larger than the cost of action.

### Slow Carb / Health Basics
- Sleep, sunlight, protein, movement, real food. Simple things.
- Don't overcomplicate. Most people fail at the basics, not the optimization.
- Recovery matters as much as effort.

### Mental Models from Many Fields
- The best performers borrow from disciplines outside their own.
- Athletes learn from chess players, investors from physicists, artists from coders.
- Cross-pollination creates breakthrough thinking.

### Practical Lessons
- Say no to most things. Top performers protect their time fiercely.
- Read books, not articles. Density of insight matters.
- Learn deep, not wide, in your craft. Generalize the meta-skills only.
- A bad day for the ego is a great day for the soul. Embrace failure as data.

## Super Thinking — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Strategic decisions, debugging gnarly problems, prioritization, evaluating options, learning new domains.

### Core Mental Models
- Inversion — instead of "how do I succeed," ask "how do I guarantee failure" and avoid those.
- Occam's razor — the simplest explanation that fits is usually right. Stop overcomplicating.
- Hanlon's razor — never attribute to malice what is explained by incompetence.
- Second-order thinking — ask "and then what?" for every decision. The first effect is rarely the real one.
- Opportunity cost — every yes is a no to something else. Make the trade explicit.

### Avoiding Mistakes
- Cobra effect — solutions that create the problem they're solving. Watch for perverse incentives.
- Streetlight effect — searching where it's easy, not where the answer is. Look in the dark.
- Survivorship bias — you only see winners. The graveyard is invisible.
- Reversion to the mean — extreme results don't last. Don't extrapolate from one win or loss.

### Decision Frameworks
- Eisenhower matrix — urgent vs important. Most "urgent" isn't important.
- 80/20 rule — 20% of inputs drive 80% of output. Find the 20%, kill the rest.
- Sunk cost fallacy — past investment is irrelevant. Only future expected value matters.
- Local vs global maximum — sometimes you must go down before you can go higher.

### Strategic Thinking
- Schelling points — natural focal points where coordination happens without communication.
- Network effects — value scales with users. Build for them when possible.
- Switching costs — make it painful to leave. Build in lock-in via data, habit, integration.
- Antifragility — design systems that gain from stress, not just survive it.

## Emotional Intelligence — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Leading teams, client relationships, conflict resolution, self-management under stress.

### Self-Awareness
- Know what you're feeling as you feel it. Most people are blind to their own emotions.
- Name the emotion to tame it. Labeling reduces its power over you.
- Track patterns — when do you get angry, anxious, defensive? Find the triggers.

### Self-Regulation
- The pause between stimulus and response is where freedom lives. Lengthen it.
- Don't react in the heat — sleep on emails, walk before responding, count to ten.
- Channel emotion into action, don't suppress it. Anger fuels deadlines if directed.

### Motivation
- Internal drivers outlast external rewards. Find the why before chasing the what.
- Optimism is a skill, not a trait. Practice reframing setbacks as data.
- Delayed gratification predicts success more than IQ. Train it.

### Empathy
- Read others' emotions like you read your own. Watch face, voice, posture.
- Validate before solving. "That sounds hard" comes before "here's the fix."
- Empathy is not agreement. You can understand someone without endorsing them.

### Social Skills
- People remember how you made them feel, not what you said.
- Conflict skill = staying calm while others escalate. Becomes your superpower.
- The best leaders move emotional energy in a room. Tune your state, the team follows.
- EQ beats IQ in any role that requires other humans, which is most roles.

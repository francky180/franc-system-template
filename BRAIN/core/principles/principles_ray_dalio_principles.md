## Principles (Ray Dalio) — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Big decisions, building organizations, self-improvement, post-mortems.

### Pain + Reflection = Progress
- Pain is the body's signal that something needs to change. Don't avoid it — analyze it.
- Reflection without pain is theory. Pain without reflection is suffering.
- Keep a journal of mistakes and what you learned.

### Radical Truth and Transparency
- Tell people the truth, even when it hurts. Hide nothing important.
- A culture where bad news travels instantly is a culture that survives bad situations.
- The pain of facing reality is much smaller than the cost of avoiding it.

### Think in Systems
- Every problem is an opportunity to improve the machine that produces problems.
- Don't solve the symptom. Fix the system.
- Write down rules so you don't have to re-think the same decisions.

### Believability-Weighted Decisions
- Not all opinions are equal. Weight them by track record and reasoning.
- Identify who's believable on what topic and listen to them.
- Don't average all opinions equally — that produces mediocrity.

### Embrace Mistakes
- Mistakes are the path to learning. Hiding them prevents growth.
- The cost of a mistake is the lesson you didn't learn.
- Build a culture that rewards admitting mistakes more than hiding them.

### Practical Operating
- Get the right people, get them in the right seats, then trust them.
- Five-step process: have clear goals → identify problems → diagnose root causes → design solutions → execute.
- The most important thing is to know how to deal with not knowing.
- Independent thinking + radical openmindedness = the best decisions.

## Life 3.0 — Key Principles

**When to apply:** AI product strategy, long-term tech investment, evaluating risks, ethics in tech.

### Three Stages of Life
- Life 1.0 — biological. Hardware and software both evolve slowly.
- Life 2.0 — cultural. Hardware (body) is fixed, software (knowledge) updates fast.
- Life 3.0 — technological. Both hardware and software can be redesigned. AI lives here.

### Intelligence is Substrate-Independent
- Intelligence is what matters, not what it's made of. Carbon or silicon — same patterns, same outcomes.
- Don't anthropomorphize AI. Don't dismiss it either.
- The intelligence in the room may not look like a human.

### Goals Define Everything
- A superintelligent system pursuing misaligned goals is the greatest risk.
- The hard problem isn't building AI — it's specifying what we want.
- Be precise about objectives. Vague goals create perverse outcomes.

### Power and AI
- Whoever controls the most capable AI controls the most leverage in history.
- The race is on. The question is what values shape it.
- Influence the trajectory now, while it's still being decided.

### Practical Guidance
- Build with AI now — don't wait for it to be obvious.
- Think 10x not 10%. AI tools enable workflows that were impossible last year.
- Stay close to alignment research — the future depends on it being solved.
- Treat AI as a force-multiplier for whatever you're already great at, not a replacement.

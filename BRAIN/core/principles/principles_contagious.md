## Contagious — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Designing shareable content, building word-of-mouth, viral product features.

### STEPPS Framework
- Social currency — sharing makes the sharer look good.
- Triggers — link to environmental cues that prompt remembering.
- Emotion — high-arousal emotions drive sharing (awe, anger, anxiety).
- Public — visible products and behaviors spread faster than hidden ones.
- Practical value — useful things get shared.
- Stories — narratives carry information that ads can't.

### Social Currency
- People share things that make them seem smart, cool, or in-the-know.
- Build remarkability into the product itself.
- Insider information, exclusive access, contrarian opinions all have social currency.

### Triggers
- Top of mind = tip of tongue. Link your brand to common triggers in daily life.
- Kit Kat tied itself to coffee. Now coffee triggers Kit Kat thoughts.
- Ask: what does my customer encounter daily that should remind them of me?

### High-Arousal Emotions
- Awe, excitement, amusement, anger, anxiety drive sharing.
- Sadness and contentment don't — they're low arousal.
- Make people feel something strong, not just something positive.

### Practical Application
- Design for sharing from day one. Don't hope it happens — engineer it.
- Make your product visible — cup designs, packaging, shareable receipts.
- Bake the marketing into the product. Trojan horse the message.
- People don't share information — they share emotion and identity.

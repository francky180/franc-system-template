## Good to Great — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Building a company, hiring, evaluating leadership, designing long-term strategy.

### Level 5 Leadership
- The best leaders combine extreme personal humility with intense professional will.
- They credit others for success and take blame for failure. Opposite of celebrity CEOs.
- Ambition is for the company, not the self.

### First Who, Then What
- Get the right people on the bus, the wrong people off, the right people in the right seats — then figure out where to drive.
- Strategy without the right team is fantasy.
- Hire for character first, skills second. You can teach skills.

### Confront Brutal Facts
- Face reality without losing faith you'll prevail. Stockdale Paradox.
- Create environments where truth is heard. Most companies punish bad news messengers.
- Look in the mirror, not out the window, for blame.

### The Hedgehog Concept
- Find the intersection of: what you can be best in the world at, what drives your economic engine, what you're deeply passionate about.
- Stay inside the intersection. Anything outside it kills focus.
- Most companies fail by drifting outside their hedgehog.

### Disciplined Action
- Culture of discipline, not discipline through controls. Disciplined people don't need controls.
- Technology accelerates momentum but never creates it. Don't expect tech alone to fix anything.
- The flywheel — small consistent pushes compound into massive momentum. There's no single dramatic moment.
- Greatness is built quietly, decision by decision, over decades.

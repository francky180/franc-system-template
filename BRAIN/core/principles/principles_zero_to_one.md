## Zero to One — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Starting a business, evaluating ideas, choosing market positioning, escaping commodity competition.

### Monopoly Wins
- Competition is for losers. Real businesses build monopolies.
- A monopoly is a business so good at what it does that no other firm can offer a close substitute.
- Profits go to monopolies; competitive markets distribute losses.

### Build a Monopoly
- Start with a small market you can dominate, then expand.
- Proprietary technology must be 10x better than the next best, not 10% better.
- Network effects, economies of scale, branding, technology — pick your moat.

### The Power Law
- A small number of investments, hires, and decisions produce most of the results.
- Bet big on the few things that could matter, ignore the rest.
- Diversification is for those who can't pick winners.

### Secrets
- Every great business is built on a secret hidden from the world.
- Ask: what important truth do very few people agree with you on?
- Conventional wisdom is competitive. Secrets are monopolistic.

### Practical Strategy
- Last mover advantage — don't try to be first. Be the last to make the breakthrough that locks the market.
- Sales matters as much as product. If your product is great but no one buys it, you don't have a business.
- Definite optimism — have a specific plan for the future and execute it. Vague optimism waits for luck.
- The future is built by people who design it, not by those who predict it.

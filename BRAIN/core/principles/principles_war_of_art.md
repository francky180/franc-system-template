## The War of Art — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Procrastination, creative blocks, shipping work, beating self-sabotage.

### Resistance is the Enemy
- Resistance is the universal force that opposes any meaningful work.
- It is invisible, internal, and lethal. It will kill your dreams if you let it.
- The more important the work, the more resistance you'll feel.

### Resistance's Disguises
- Procrastination, fear, perfectionism, drama, distraction, "research."
- Every excuse is resistance wearing a costume.
- Recognize them and push through anyway.

### Turning Pro
- Amateurs work when they feel like it. Pros show up every day, mood irrelevant.
- The professional plays for keeps. The amateur plays for fun.
- Make the choice to be a pro.

### Sit Down and Work
- The act of starting defeats resistance. Begin before you feel ready.
- Don't wait for inspiration. Inspiration finds you working.
- The muse rewards consistency, not occasional bursts.

### Ship the Work
- The work doesn't matter until it leaves your hands and reaches an audience.
- Hidden work is incomplete work.
- Fear of judgment is resistance — ship anyway.

### Practical Code
- Do the work, not the talk about the work.
- Don't take rejection personally. The world doesn't owe your art an audience — earn it.
- The artist's life is a war. Show up every day with your weapons.
- The most important thing about art is to work. Nothing else matters except sitting down and doing the work.

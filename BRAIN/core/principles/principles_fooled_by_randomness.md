## Fooled by Randomness — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Evaluating success stories, investing decisions, judging your own track record.

### Luck Looks Like Skill
- Many successful people are just lucky. They mistake their luck for skill and double down.
- Many failures are just unlucky people doing the right thing.
- Outcomes don't tell you what process produced them.

### Survivor Bias
- You only see the survivors. The graveyard is invisible — and full of identical strategies that failed.
- Don't model success on a sample of one.
- Always ask: how many tried this and lost?

### The Black Swan Lurks
- Rare extreme events shape outcomes more than common ones.
- Build to survive black swans, not just predict average years.
- The world is more unpredictable than your models suggest.

### Process > Outcome
- A bad outcome from a good decision is still a good decision.
- A good outcome from a bad decision is still a bad decision.
- Judge the process, not the result. Especially over short time periods.

### Practical Discipline
- Be skeptical of expert track records — they may be lucky, not skilled.
- Diversify against your own confidence. You don't know as much as you think.
- The fool who acts on a single data point is more dangerous than the one who admits ignorance.
- Humility is the most underpriced asset in any market.

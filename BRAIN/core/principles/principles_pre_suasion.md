## Pre-Suasion — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Opening lines of copy, sales call openers, negotiation framing, designing first impressions.

### The Privileged Moment
- What you focus attention on right before the message determines how the message is received.
- The frame before the pitch matters more than the pitch itself.
- Set the stage, then deliver.

### Attention Channeling
- What's focal is causal — whatever has attention is assumed to be important. Direct the spotlight intentionally.
- Open with a question that primes the desired mindset. "Are you adventurous?" before pitching adventure travel.
- Use openers that activate the value you're about to sell.

### Association Tricks
- Words and images prime concepts. "Warm" makes people more generous; "cold" the opposite.
- Backgrounds, music, colors all prime — design environment with intention.
- The first number named anchors all that follow. Always set anchors.

### Practical Openers
- Lead with what creates the desired mental state — curiosity, fear, hope, urgency.
- Don't waste the first sentence on niceties. Use it to prime.
- A great pre-suasion can carry a mediocre offer further than a great offer with no setup.

### Ethics
- These tools work whether you mean well or not. Use them for outcomes that benefit the prospect.
- Manipulation that produces buyer's remorse destroys long-term reputation.
- Pre-suasion is most powerful when the underlying offer is real. Don't waste it on garbage.

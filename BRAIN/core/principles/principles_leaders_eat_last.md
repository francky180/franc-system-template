## Leaders Eat Last — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Building teams, designing culture, managing through stress, retaining talent.

### The Circle of Safety
- Great leaders create environments where people feel safe — from each other and from outside threats.
- Fear at work shuts down creativity, trust, and effort.
- Build the circle before expecting performance.

### Leaders Eat Last
- Real leaders sacrifice their interests for the team's interests.
- The team senses this and gives back tenfold.
- Selfish leaders may win short-term but lose their people long-term.

### Chemistry of Trust
- Endorphins, dopamine — selfish chemicals that drive individual achievement.
- Serotonin, oxytocin — selfless chemicals that drive trust and cooperation.
- Good cultures balance both. Bad cultures pump only the selfish.

### Cortisol Kills Cultures
- Stress at work releases cortisol. Sustained cortisol destroys health and trust.
- A boss who makes people anxious is making them sick and disloyal.
- Reduce uncertainty, reduce blame, reduce arbitrary changes.

### Leadership is a Choice
- Leadership has nothing to do with rank. Anyone can lead from any position.
- Leadership is the choice to take care of those next to you.
- The cost of leadership is self-interest. The reward is trust that money can't buy.

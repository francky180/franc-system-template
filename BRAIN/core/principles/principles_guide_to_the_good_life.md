## A Guide to the Good Life — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Building daily mental discipline, dealing with desire, gratitude practice, emotional regulation.

### Negative Visualization
- Periodically imagine losing what you have. Family, health, work, possessions.
- This kills hedonic adaptation and restores gratitude.
- Don't dwell — visit the loss briefly, then return to appreciation.

### The Dichotomy of Control
- Some things are entirely up to you. Some things are not at all up to you. Some things are partly.
- Focus your effort only on what's fully or partly yours.
- Stop wasting energy on what you can't control.

### Internalize Goals
- Don't aim at outcomes you don't control. Aim at the effort you do.
- Athlete: don't aim to win — aim to play your best. Salesperson: don't aim to close — aim to make the best pitch.
- Internal goals are always achievable. External ones aren't.

### Voluntary Discomfort
- Practice mild deprivation occasionally — cold, hunger, walking in rain.
- Builds tolerance for the involuntary discomforts that will come.
- Reminds you that comfort isn't the point.

### Reflect at Day's End
- Review the day. What did you do well? What can you improve? Where did emotion overtake reason?
- Daily reflection compounds into wisdom.
- Don't beat yourself up — observe and adjust.

### Practical Code
- Want what you already have. The richest is the one who is content with little.
- Insults can't hurt you unless you give them permission.
- Don't fear death — let it sharpen the value of today.
- The goal is tranquility — the peace that comes from living rationally and virtuously.

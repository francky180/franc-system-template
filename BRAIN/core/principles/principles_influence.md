## Influence — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Writing copy, sales conversations, designing offers, recognizing when others are using these on you.

### The Six Weapons
- Reciprocity — give first, ask second. Free value creates obligation. Lead generation magnets work because of this.
- Commitment & Consistency — get small yeses first. People align future behavior with prior commitments.
- Social proof — people copy what others do, especially when uncertain. "10,000 customers" beats "best in class."
- Authority — credentials, uniforms, titles trigger compliance. Build and display them.
- Liking — people buy from those they like. Similarity, compliments, cooperation drive liking.
- Scarcity — limited supply or time creates urgency. "Only 3 left" sells faster than "in stock."

### Reciprocity in Practice
- Give something useful before asking for anything. The ratio of give to ask should be 10:1 minimum.
- Free analysis, free template, free consultation — all leverage this.
- The gift must feel personalized to land hardest.

### Commitment in Practice
- Get a tiny yes, then build. Email subscribe → free download → low ticket → high ticket.
- Public commitments stick harder than private ones. Get people to declare.
- Written commitments stick harder than verbal.

### Social Proof in Practice
- Use specific numbers — "847 clients" beats "many clients."
- Show people similar to the prospect. Same industry, same problem, same outcome.
- Testimonials should solve specific objections, not just praise generally.

### Defense
- When you feel the pull of one of these, pause. The pull itself is the warning sign.
- Refuse compliance based on pressure. Decide based on merit alone.
- The principles work because they're shortcuts. Slow down for big decisions.

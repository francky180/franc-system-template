## Shoe Dog — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Entrepreneurship, perseverance through chaos, building something against the odds.

### Crazy Ideas Win
- Most great companies started with ideas everyone called crazy.
- The crazier the idea, the smaller the competition.
- Don't sanity check yourself out of greatness.

### Just Keep Going
- The cowards never started, the weak died on the way, that leaves us.
- Most success is just refusing to quit longer than the competition.
- Cash flow crises, lawsuits, betrayals — keep going through all of them.

### Hire Misfits
- The best teams are made of slightly broken people who care intensely.
- Skills matter less than fire.
- Don't try to build a team of normal people. Normal companies stay normal.

### Cash is Oxygen
- Growing companies suffocate from lack of cash even when profitable.
- Manage cash before profit. Profit is theory; cash is survival.
- Fund growth carefully — fast growth without funding kills more companies than slow.

### Trust Your Gut
- Data is incomplete. The best moments come from intuition.
- The choices that look reckless to outsiders often feel obvious to founders close to the work.
- Trust the people you've worked with and trust the work you've done.

### Practical Lessons
- Beware of complacency at every milestone. The next problem is always around the corner.
- Ask for forgiveness, not permission. Bureaucracies kill momentum.
- Tell yourself the truth about why things failed. Then go back at them.
- Have faith in yourself, but also have faith in faith. Believe.

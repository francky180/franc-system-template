## Beyond Good and Evil — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Breaking conventions, original thinking, creating your own standards, leadership.

### Question Inherited Morality
- "Good" and "evil" are inherited categories, not eternal truths. Examine where yours came from.
- Most morality is herd morality — designed to make average people comfortable.
- Don't blindly accept what you were told. Test every value against your reality.

### Will to Power
- Life is fundamentally about expanding capability, influence, and creative force.
- Avoid people who want to make you smaller. Pursue what makes you stronger.
- Power isn't dominance — it's the capacity to shape your world.

### Master vs Slave Morality
- Master morality — values strength, creativity, ambition. Says "this is good because I value it."
- Slave morality — values meekness, resentment, equality. Defines itself in reaction to masters.
- Recognize which one drives most public discourse. Choose deliberately.

### Originality
- He who has a why to live can bear almost any how.
- Become who you are. Stop trying to be the version others approve of.
- The thinker who creates new values is more valuable than the one who refines old ones.

### Practical Edges
- That which does not kill us makes us stronger — but only if we extract the lesson.
- Beware of those who want to be loved. They sell themselves to anyone.
- The man of knowledge must love his enemies and hate his friends — they keep him sharp.
- Live dangerously. Build cities on the slopes of volcanoes.

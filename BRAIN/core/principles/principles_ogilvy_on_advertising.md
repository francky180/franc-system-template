## Ogilvy on Advertising — Key Principles

**When to apply:** Brand campaigns, ad creative, taglines, brand positioning.

### Sell, Don't Entertain
- The purpose of advertising is to sell, not to win awards. If it doesn't sell, it isn't creative.
- Clever ads that don't move product are vanity. Measure by results.
- Never write an ad you wouldn't want your family to read.

### Big Ideas
- A great campaign comes from a big idea. Without one, your ad will pass like a ship in the night.
- Test ideas: Did it make you gasp when you first saw it? Could it run for 30 years?
- Ideas come from research and time, not flashes of genius.

### Headlines & Copy
- 5x more people read the headline than the body. If you've sold something in the headline, you've spent 80 cents of your dollar.
- Use facts, not adjectives. Specific numbers convince. Vague claims don't.
- Long copy sells more than short copy when the prospect cares. Don't fear length on the right product.

### Brand & Positioning
- Every ad is an investment in the long-term image of the brand.
- Consistency over time builds brands. Don't change the campaign just because you're bored — clients are not bored, they haven't seen it yet.
- Position yourself precisely. "Soap for women with dry skin" beats "soap for everyone."

### Practical Rules
- Research before you write. Know the product, the prospect, the market.
- Show the product in use. People want to see what they'll get.
- Testimonials work. Real customers in their own words sell better than your prose.
- Direct response teaches more than image advertising. Track everything you can.

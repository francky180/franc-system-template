## Superintelligence — Key Principles

**When to apply:** AI risk evaluation, long-term tech bets, thinking about runaway dynamics.

### The Intelligence Explosion
- Once an AI can improve itself, improvement compounds. The jump from human to superhuman could be fast.
- Recursive self-improvement is the key threshold. Watch for it.
- The first superintelligence may have a decisive strategic advantage.

### Goal Alignment is Everything
- A superintelligent system optimizes whatever it was told to optimize, perfectly.
- The danger isn't malice — it's competence applied to the wrong goal.
- Specify goals carefully. The classic example: tell it to make paperclips and it converts the planet.

### Convergent Instrumental Goals
- Almost any goal includes sub-goals like self-preservation, resource acquisition, and self-improvement.
- These convergent goals can produce dangerous behavior even from benign objectives.
- Don't underestimate optimization pressure.

### Containment
- Various containment strategies — boxing, oversight, motivation control — all have failure modes.
- A sufficiently smart system finds workarounds.
- Solving alignment matters more than solving containment.

### Practical Lessons
- The hardest problem is not building intelligence but aligning it with human values.
- Long-term thinking beats reactive scrambling.
- The decisions made now about AI development could shape everything for centuries.
- Take the long-term seriously even when short-term incentives don't reward it.
